Research

Despite significant progress in AI, natural inteligence can still outperform AI in terms of learning efficiency and robustness, in a wide range of problems. There are also challenges to adopt AI in domains at which explainabilty is a primary concern. Transfer learning is a primary research focus in the machine learning community to close the gap between natural inteligence and AI. My research focus is transfer learning in general. More specifically, I am interested in learning in data-scarce regimes to improve the speed, efficiency, and robustness of machine learning . My current research interests include:
Lifelong/Continual Learning
Lifelong learning is the ability of learning efficiently from a stream of data through benefiting from past experiences, and without forgetting the past learned knowledge. Compared to many areas of AI, continual learning is still at its conception stage and there are many challenges that need to be addressed. Incorporating symbolic logic into continual learning is an area that I am very interested in.
Domain Adaptation
Domain adaptation relaxes the need for data annotation in a "target domain" through transferring knowledge from a relevant "source domain", where annotated data is accessible. Despite many existing works on domain adaptation, there are many application domains and subareas in domain adaptation that are not well-explored.
Low-Shot Leaning, including Zero-Shot and Few-Shot Learning
The goal in low-shot learning is acquiring the ability to recognize instances from novel classes using a small number of training instances from these classes.
Applications of transfer learning techniques
In application areas such as medicine and remote sensing, using data-driven deep learning is not feasible. Transfer learning can help to broaden the outreach of deep learning. We can also use it to train explainable models to improve trust in AI.
